Kia ora Everyone!

What a weekend, phew! I need a few more days to get over the last four. We were up at 5am on Saturday to catch the first water taxi from Kawau Island so we could drive down to Papamoa and dog sit for my parents while they came up to Auckland for a couple of days. Getting to the pontoon in the dark was a mission. The dinghy we borrowed (ours had to be stowed on the boat) rowed like a cork, and it had a small leak, so with the two of us and all our stuff aboard it was sitting low in the water. I had to perch right behind Eben so the bow didn't go under! We tried to keep quiet, but between Eben cussing, me trying to give directions in the dark, and the noisy oarlocks, we're sure we woke everyone up. The sun was just starting to rise as we reached the pontoon. What a start to our Saturday!

Once safely on the other side, we hopped in the car, stopped for a quick coffee and breakfast, then drove to Auckland to grab some things from our storage unit before getting back on the road for the next 2-3 hours to my parents' place. They hopped in their car and headed up so Mum could celebrate her birthday with her old school friends while Dad visited with his. We spent the last couple of days looking after their cute as Rottweiler, Henry (biggest teddy bear ever), and Lab-Collie cross, Stanley (a bundle of nervous energy). My parents drove back yesterday and we did the swap again, this time to start our two-month house/cat-sitting duties for our friends. I get to swap dog cuddles for cat cuddles, so I'm good. Eben has already set up the "work-til-I-drop" station that will be his spot for the next two months! Now to spend the next day or so catching our breath and catching up on sleep.

Hope your weekend involved fewer leaky dinghies and a lot more sleep, but was just as awesome!
