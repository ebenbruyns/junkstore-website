**This Week's Pro Tip:** Want to play your Epic games without an internet connection? Junk Store makes it easy to enable offline mode.

<h2 style="text-align: left !important; margin-left: 0;">The Problem</h2>

Epic games, once downloaded, tend to 'phone home' to the Epic servers when launched. The problem arises when you try to launch games while not connected to the internet. They can't reach Epic's servers, so they refuse to launch. You can easily fix this in Junk Store by toggling offline mode for games that support this feature.

The setup differs slightly between versions, so here's how to do it on both.

<h2 style="text-align: left !important; margin-left: 0;">Decky Plugin: Global Setting</h2>

On the free Decky plugin, offline mode is a **global setting** that applies to all your Epic games.

<ol style="padding-left: 1.2em; margin-left: 0; text-align: left;">
<li>Open <strong>Junk Store</strong> and go to the <strong>Epic</strong> tab</li>
<li>Press the <strong>cog icon</strong> (settings)</li>
</ol>

![Epic tab cog settings](/assets/images/blog/tips/offline-tip/epictabcog.webp){: .align-center loading="lazy"}

<ol start="3" style="padding-left: 1.2em; margin-left: 0; text-align: left;">
<li>Toggle <strong>Offline Mode</strong> on and remember to <strong>Push X to Save</strong></li>
</ol>

![Global offline mode setting](/assets/images/blog/tips/offline-tip/globalofflineon.webp){: .align-center loading="lazy"}

That's it. Your Epic games that support it will now launch in offline mode.

<div class="tip-callout">
  <strong>Note:</strong> This is a global setting that applies to all Epic games, whether they support offline mode or not. Games that don't support it will refuse to launch while this is enabled. If a game won't start, remember to check this setting and toggle it off.
</div>

<h2 style="text-align: left !important; margin-left: 0;">Junk Store 2.0: Per-Game Setting</h2>

Junk Store 2.0 also has the global setting like the Decky plugin, but if you want more control you can set offline mode on a **per-game basis**.

<ol style="padding-left: 1.2em; margin-left: 0; text-align: left;">
<li>Open the <strong>game page</strong> for your Epic game</li>
<li>Press the <strong>cog icon</strong> (settings)</li>
<li>Select <strong>Game Configs</strong></li>
</ol>

![Game Configs menu](/assets/images/blog/tips/offline-tip/2.0gameconfig.webp){: .align-center loading="lazy"}

<ol start="4" style="padding-left: 1.2em; margin-left: 0; text-align: left;">
<li>Toggle <strong>Offline Mode</strong> on and remember to <strong>Push X to Save</strong></li>
</ol>

![Offline Mode toggle](/assets/images/blog/tips/offline-tip/2.0offline.webp){: .align-center loading="lazy"}

The setting saves automatically for that game. Repeat for any other games you want to play offline.

<div class="tip-callout">
  <strong>Pro Tip:</strong> Great for single-player games you want to play on the go, while keeping online games connected.
</div>

<h2 style="text-align: left !important; margin-left: 0;">Before You Go Offline</h2>

A few things to keep in mind:

- **Launch the game once while online** before going offline. This ensures any required tokens are cached.
- **Some games require online authentication** and won't work offline regardless of this setting.
- **DRM-free games** (like those from GOG) don't need this, they work offline by default.

Hope this helps for your next offline adventure!
